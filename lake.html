A Quiet Lake…

Lake Auger is a relatively small classic Adirondack lake. It is a shallow lake; only reaching depths slightly over 20 feet. It covers roughly 350 acres.

Most of the development on the lake is along the north-western shore. Two summer camps are located along the north shore. The camp at the Anchorage offers views of the undeveloped south side of the lake.

Guided Fishing at Lake Auger…

Lake Auger offers great fishing for many species of fish. Panfish, large and small-mouth bass, pike, perch and even carp populate the lake.

Make sure to bring your fishing gear, or borrow some of ours here at the camp.

Plenty to Do…

There is plenty of great swimming, fishing, and boating to be done at Auger Lake. We have a small dock with a canoe, a kayak, and even a vintage john boat with a even more vintage motor. Still cranks on the first pull!

The low rock, the medium rock, or the high rock; you decide! There numerous places to "jump" in at Auger Lake.

The undeveloped shore across the lake is open to the public. There are a couple of fantastic fire pits and even a lean-to to camp in.
